# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Semikons Embedded Technologies is committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and respectful environment for all community members, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual identity and orientation, or socioeconomic status.

We believe that diverse perspectives and experiences make our community stronger and our projects better.

---

## Our Standards

### ✅ Expected Behavior

Community members are expected to:

- **Be Respectful**: Treat all members with dignity and respect
- **Be Inclusive**: Welcome people of all backgrounds and experiences
- **Be Collaborative**: Work constructively toward shared goals
- **Be Professional**: Maintain a level of professionalism in all interactions
- **Be Patient**: Understand that people have different expertise levels
- **Provide Constructive Feedback**: Offer helpful criticism focused on ideas, not people
- **Acknowledge Mistakes**: Apologize sincerely and commit to improvement
- **Respect Privacy**: Do not share personal information without consent
- **Use Clear Communication**: Avoid ambiguous or inflammatory language
- **Support Others**: Help newcomers and less experienced contributors

### ❌ Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are prohibited:

1. **Harassment & Bullying**
   - Sexual harassment, unwelcome advances, or inappropriate comments
   - Intimidation, threats, or aggressive behavior
   - Deliberately upsetting or attacking others
   - Persistent, targeted criticism unrelated to work quality

2. **Discrimination**
   - Comments or actions based on protected characteristics (race, gender, etc.)
   - Exclusion or isolation based on background or identity
   - Offensive stereotypes or generalizations
   - Denying opportunities based on discriminatory reasons

3. **Abuse & Violations of Dignity**
   - Slurs, profanity, or dehumanizing language
   - Personal attacks or name-calling
   - Mocking others' appearance, identity, or beliefs
   - Sharing others' personal information without consent (doxxing)

4. **Disruption**
   - Spam, trolling, or off-topic discussions
   - Repeatedly derailing conversations
   - Flooding with repetitive messages
   - Intentionally creating confusion or conflict

5. **Illegal or Unethical Conduct**
   - Sharing malicious code or security exploits
   - Copyright infringement or plagiarism
   - Attempting to compromise security or privacy
   - Any conduct that violates applicable laws

6. **Retaliation**
   - Punishing or threatening someone for reporting violations
   - Creating hostile environments for reporters
   - Spreading rumors or false information about reporters

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- **All interactions** in Semikons GitHub repositories (issues, pull requests, discussions, wiki)
- **All public communications** representing Semikons (social media, forums, events)
- **All private communications** between community members related to projects
- **Offline events** organized by or representing Semikons

This Code of Conduct applies equally to founders, maintainers, contributors, and users.

---

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you witness or experience a violation, please report it:

1. **For Minor Issues**: Address directly with the person if comfortable doing so
2. **For Serious Issues**: Contact the Semikons moderation team:
   - **Email**: [admin@semikonstech.com](mailto:admin@semikonstech.com)
   - **Private Report Form**: [Link to Google Form or equivalent]
   - **GitHub Admin**: Flag via repository admin contact

### Reporting Guidelines

When reporting, please provide:
- **Description**: What happened and why it violates the Code of Conduct
- **Location**: Link to the issue, PR, comment, or other content
- **Date/Time**: When the incident occurred
- **Witnesses**: Who else was present (if applicable)
- **Impact**: How this affected you or the community
- **Previous Reports**: Any prior incidents with this person

**All reports are confidential.** We will not disclose reporter identity without permission.

### Response Process

1. **Acknowledgment**: We will acknowledge receipt within 48 hours
2. **Investigation**: Team reviews all available information
3. **Decision**: We determine if a violation occurred and appropriate action
4. **Notification**: All parties are informed of the outcome
5. **Appeal**: Parties may request reconsideration if they disagree

**Timeline**: Most decisions are made within 7 days for clear violations, up to 14 days for complex cases.

---

## Consequences

Violations may result in:

| Severity | Consequence |
|----------|-------------|
| **Minor** | Warning, comment removal, private discussion |
| **Moderate** | Temporary suspension (1-7 days) from discussions/PRs |
| **Serious** | Extended suspension (1-3 months) or permanent ban |
| **Egregious** | Immediate permanent ban, potential legal action |

**First-Time Violations**: Lesser consequences apply unless behavior is severe or threatening.

**Repeated Violations**: Consequences escalate regardless of context.

### Examples of Escalation

- **First warning**: "This language is inappropriate. Please refer to our Code of Conduct."
- **Second violation**: Comment removal + temporary discussion ban (24-48 hours)
- **Third violation**: Suspension from repository (1-2 weeks)
- **Fourth+ violations**: Permanent ban from organization

---

## Special Cases

### Accidental Violations
- **Intent**: We recognize good-faith mistakes
- **Response**: Education + warning rather than punishment
- **Recovery**: Person is reinstated after acknowledging the mistake

### Mental Health Crises
- **Intent**: Mental health challenges do not excuse harmful behavior
- **Response**: We balance compassion with community safety
- **Support**: Encourage professional help while maintaining boundaries

### Power Imbalances
- **Intent**: Maintainers and authority figures held to higher standards
- **Response**: Consequences may be more severe due to position of influence
- **Transparency**: Public disclosure of maintainer violations (with privacy considerations)

---

## Appeals Process

If you believe a decision was unjust:

1. **Submit Appeal**: Email [admin@semikonstech.com](mailto:admin@semikonstech.com) within 7 days
2. **Provide New Evidence**: Explain why the decision should be reconsidered
3. **Review by Different Member**: A different team member reviews your appeal
4. **Final Decision**: Appeal decision is final and communicated within 7 days

---

## Transparency & Accountability

### Public Reporting
- Violations are tracked in a log (without naming individuals initially)
- Patterns are identified and addressed systemically
- Annual reports summarize enforcement actions (aggregate data)

### Reviewer Accountability
- Multiple people review serious violations to prevent bias
- Conflicts of interest are disclosed
- Appeal options exist for all decisions
